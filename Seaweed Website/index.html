Seagrass

Seagrasses are unique flowering plants that exclusively grow in the sea. There are approximately 60 different species of fully marine seagrasses, all categorized into four families within the order Alismatales. These plants originated from land-based plants that adapted to the marine environment around 70 to 100 million years ago.

Seagrasses play a crucial role in the marine ecosystem, often referred to as "the lungs of the sea." This is because they release oxygen into the water through the process of photosynthesis, similar to how trees produce oxygen on land. They thrive in shallow, protected coastal waters with soft bottoms, and they can be found in both tropical and temperate regions. Essentially, seagrasses are essential contributors to the health and balance of coastal marine environments.
